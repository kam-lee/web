Research Focus
PNS

CFD has become an essential tool in hypersonic technology research. It has the advantages of a short development cycle and small cost, and it can simulate many working environments that cannot be achieved by experiments. At present, in the field of CFD, the time iteration method based on Full Navier-Stokes (FNS) equations can meet the needs of most engineering problems in hypersonic technology research in terms of accuracy. Unfortunately, however, such CFD methods generally take a long computational time.

To solve this problem, The LU-SGS implicit time integration method is successfully extended to the flow space integration of the PNS equation. A single-scan spatial advancement algorithm, SSPNS, based on the PNS equations is developed, in which the transverse inviscid numerical fluxes and viscous fluxes are solved by the hybrid windward format and the central format, respectively.

By comparison with the traditional time-iterative Full Navier-Stokes (FNS) flow solvers, the SSPNS codes show 1~2 orders of magnitude of computational speed faster and at least 1 order of magnitude of storage-saving in the 3D hypersonic inlet flow field simulation. All the numerical results indicate that SSPNS is a highly efficient, highly accurate, and also highly robust algorithm for steady supersonic/hypersonic flows without any large streamwise separation, and it is appropriate to be used in the aerodynamic optimization design of scramjet inlet and nozzle.

This group combined optimization methods such as Sequential Quadratic Programming (SQP), Multi-Island Genetic Algorithm (MIGA), and Multi-Objective Genetic Algorithm (NCGA and NSGA-II) with the SSPNS flow field computation method to investigate the optimal design of the nozzle and inlet.
