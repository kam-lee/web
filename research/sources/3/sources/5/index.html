Research Focus
Two-phase Flow

With the rapid increase in the Mach number of missile flight, the demand for the specific impulse performance of the engine is getting higher and higher, which puts forward higher requirements for the calorific value of the fuel, especially the volumetric calorific value. Compared with traditional liquid fuels, metal fuels have excellent energy characteristics. In addition, under higher Mach number flight conditions, the combustion products of traditional hydrocarbon fuels may dissociate due to the high temperature in the combustor, hence it's not suitable for scramjet engines. Hydrogen fuel, on the other hand, is difficult to store and has a lower volumetric calorific value. In comparison, metal fuels do not have these problems, and the application of metal fuels can improve the energy characteristics of ramjet engines. Therefore, metal fuels are one of the ideal fuels for future hypersonic cruise missiles.

The injection and mixing of metal powder fuel in the combustor is a typical two-phase flow problem, and experimental research on two-phase flow can only obtain very few results, most of which are instantaneous. Therefore, numerical simulation of two-phase flow plays an indispensable role in the design and analysis of metal powder fuel engines.

Based on the gas-phase solver Hurricane, our lab has developed an Eulerian-Lagrangian two-phase flow solver, with the particle phase tracked using the Lagrangian method. The solver framework is shown in the figure below. Currently, Hurricane two-phase flow solver has went through sets of validations, and has been applied in the design of engines.
