Research Focus
High-energy-density Fuels

The energy characteristics of fuels have a significant impact on engine and vehicle performance. Increasing the mass calorific value and density (volumetric calorific value) of hydrocarbon fuels is an important way to improve their energy characteristics. Hydrocarbon fuels containing high-energy solid particles are an important solution for higher power requirements in the future. High-energy solid particles have a mass calorific value that is similar to or even better than liquid hydrocarbon fuels, and the density of the particles can be increased by more than 300 per cent compared with that of liquid fuels, so that hydrocarbon fuels containing high-energy solid particles have a more satisfactory Therefore, hydrocarbon fuels with energetic solid particles have better energy characteristics. Ground engine thermal test research is an important means to evaluate the combustion characteristics and application performance of energy-containing hydrocarbon fuels in aerospace engines, and the engine performance parameters can characterise the actual energy characteristics of energy-containing hydrocarbon fuels, and Al and B particles are ideal fuels because of their high density and high calorific value, and a series of scramjet engine tests containing hydrocarbon fuels with Al and B particles have been carried out at the hypersonic propulsion laboratory.

Tests showed that adding nano-Al and nano-B to hydrocarbon fuels brought different effects; boron's high melting and boiling point characteristics led to difficulties in ignition, and the addition of 16% Al and B particles to HD-01 fuels increased the combustion efficiency by 4.3% and decreased it by 2.3%, respectively; compared with the HD-01 pure liquid fuels, the density specific impulse HD-01+16% Al and HD-01+16% B fuels increased by 4.7% and 8.4%.

Among the two fuel systems, slurry and gel, the gelation scheme of nanoparticle-containing hydrocarbon fuels has a higher potential for engineering applications. The combustion of slurry fuels leads to significant solid-phase deposition on the internal wall surface of the engine, which corresponds to a significant increase in the wall heat flow density compared to that of pure liquid fuels, and the gelation of fuels effectively improves the wall deposition situation, and the nanoparticle additions bring about a smaller increase in the wall heat flow density, which is all conducive to engine engineering applications.

The combustion performance and comprehensive application performance of nanoparticle-containing hydrocarbon fuels in super-combustion ramjet engines are better than that of rocket engines. When applied to super-combustion ramjet engines, the combustion efficiency of nanoparticle-containing hydrocarbon fuels is basically comparable to that of pure liquid fuels or shows a small increase, and the density ratio enhancement brought by the addition of nanoparticles is more significant in scramjet engines.
